Restriction Enzyme Digestion and Sequencing

The purified plasmid DNA was digested using restriction endonucleases as shown in table 1 below. Appendix A illustrates the binding sites of the enzymes to the light chain (LC) plasmid vector (VRCO3_LK_1_5) and the heavy chain (HC) plasmid vector (VRC03-HC-1_5). After digestion, the mixture was incubated for 1.5 hours at 37°C. An undigested plasmid was mixed with a 6X Loading Dye (BioLabs, USA) to be used as a control. After incubation, the restriction mixture was run on a 1.3% gel. The undigested plasmid and a 10 000 bp GeneRuler mix DNA ladder (Thermo Scientific, Illinois, USA) were also run alongside the restriction products on the gel for 60 minutes at 90 volts (V) in 1X TAE buffer using the PowerPac™ Basic Power Supply (BioRad, USA). The gel was then immersed in a 10 000X GelRed (Biotium, USA) solution prepared according to table 2 and put on a shaker (70 rpm) for 45 minutes to post-stain. Thereafter, the band sizes were confirmed using GelDoc™ XR+ and Image Lab™ Software (BioRad, USA).

Ethanol/EDTA precipitation was used for the post-reaction cleanup. 5 μl of 125 mM EDTA and 60 μl of absolute ethanol were added to eight 1.5 ml microcentrifuge tubes. Each sequence reaction mixture was diluted to 20 μl by adding 10 μl nuclease-free water. Each diluted sequence reaction mixture was then added to an EDTA and absolute ethanol mixture. The tubes were vortexed for 60 seconds. The tubes were then left at room temperature for 15 minutes to allow for DNA to precipitate. After that, the tubes were centrifuged at 14000 x g for 20 minutes at 4°C. The supernatant was completely removed without disturbing the pellet. In each tube, 500 μl of 70% ice-cold ethanol was added, and they were centrifuged at 14 000 x g for 10 minutes at 4°C. The supernatant was removed, and the tubes were incubated at 37°C with open lids until completely dry. Thereafter, the tubes were stored at 4°C in the dark. The samples were submitted to the Department of Genetics at the University of the Free State for sequencing.
